# Decoding how the oral barrier senses, heals, and signals

The mouth is a densely colonized barrier that must distinguish friend from foe, repair constant injury, and relay information to distant tissues. Our lab investigates how local tissue organization and microbial exposure shape adaptive immunity, wound healing, and whole‑body physiology at this interface.

Using advanced imaging, single‑cell and spatial genomics, and *in vivo* models, we chart how oral immune circuits emerge, how they fail in disease, and how local inflammation at the oral barrier can influence systemic health.
